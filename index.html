A portfolio dedicated to projects of the mapped environment

The Coral Reefs of Moloka'i Island

A map of various coral reefs, mountains, and elevation/bathymetric contour data surrounding Moloka'i Island in the Hawaiian Archipelago.

The Mandirs of Varanasi

A map of the Hindu religious landscape through 'tiny temples.' A distribution of mandirs throughout the city of Varanasi and their role in the shared sacred experience.

A Map of the Uttarayan Festival in Ahmedebad, India

A map of where to find kites and experience the exciting Uttarayan Kite Festival in Ahmedebad, India in January.

An Historic Pacific Crest Trail

A map of the Pacific Crest Trail georeferenced on an historic map produced by Edwin Raisz in 1957.

Median Household Income in the United States

A map of the average income per person in each county of the United States of America.

Power Plants of the United States

A map of wood and biomass power plants throughout the United States and their corresponding energy outputs in megawatt output.

About Me

Welcome! My name is Nathaniel Deaton and I have a keen interest in geography and the mapped environment. Upon completing my BA from Centre College and MA from Tel Aviv University, I returned to Kentucky where I've been focused on data visualization and GIS. I am currently working towards a skillset in web development and digital mapping at the University of Kentucky’s MS program, where I’m applying these concepts towards fresh systems and methods for analyzing data. At the end of the day, I care about taking data and turning it into useful information.

When I’m not at my computer, you’ll find me out on the trail, making charcoal drawings, or putting flowers in holes in the backyard. For more about my work, take a look at some of my projects and feel free to drop a line.
